Ernst Haas: New York in Color 1952-1962

・BlackThe first book on master photographer Ernst Haas"s work dedicated to both his classic and newly discovered New York City color photographs of the 1950s and 60s. Ernst Haas"s color works reveal the photographer"s remarkable genius and remind us on every page why we love New York. When Haas moved from Vienna to New York City in 1951 he left behind a war-torn continent and a career producing black-and-white images. For Haas the new medium of color photography was the only way to capture a city pulsing with energy and humanity. These images demonstrate Haas"s tremendous virtuosity and confidence with Kodachrome film and the technical challenges of color printing. Unparalleled in their depth and richness of color brimming with lyricism and dramatic tension these images reveal a photographer at the height of his career.
